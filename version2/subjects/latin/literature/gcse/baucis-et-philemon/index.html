Baucis et Philemon

Ovid, Metamorphoses 8.626–724. One of two prescribed texts for Verse Literature A (cycle 4, 2025 & 2026). Sections are being added as they are finished.

  • Lesson
    Section 1 — Lines 1–11
    Jupiter and Mercury arrive in Phrygia in disguise and are turned away from a thousand homes — until one humble cottage, the home of Baucis and Philemon, takes them in.
  • Lesson
    Section 2 — Lines 12–25
    The disguised gods stoop into the cottage; old Philemon offers them a couch while busy Baucis revives the fire and the couple set about cooking a humble meal.
  • Lesson
    Section 3 — Lines 26–35
    While the meal cooks, the couple fill the wait with talk and ready a beechwood basin of warm water and a willow couch dressed for a feast day.
  • Lesson
    Section 4 — Lines 36–50
    The meal is served, and the wine bowl keeps refilling of its own accord — the terrified couple realise their guests are gods and move to sacrifice their one goose.
  • Lesson
    Section 5 — Lines 50–63
    The gods reveal themselves and lead the couple up the mountain; a flood drowns the godless region but spares the cottage, which is transformed into a temple.
  • Lesson
    Section 6 — Lines 64–76
    Asked to name their wish, the couple ask to serve as priests and to die together; in old age they are granted their prayer and turned into intertwining trees.
