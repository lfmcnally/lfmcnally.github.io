Content — what is happening
  • Before Psyche has even finished speaking, the sister — goaded by frenzied lust and poisonous jealousy — fobs off her own husband with a lie about a death in the family and races straight to the fatal crag.
  • Though the wind is against her, she gazes out in blind hope and cries: ‘Take me, Cupid, a wife worthy of you; and you, Zephyr, receive your mistress!’ — and with a great leap flings herself headlong.
  • But she cannot reach that place even in death: dashed on the rocks below, she is torn apart — a fittingly sticky end.
