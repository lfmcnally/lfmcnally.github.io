Cupid & Psyche

Apuleius, Metamorphoses VI.21–27. The set text for Prose Literature B (examination cycle 2027 & 2028). Chapters are being added as they are finished.

  • Lesson
    Chapter 21 — Psyche wavers
    Left alone with the sleeping monster she means to kill, Psyche is torn between resolve and terror — hating the beast yet loving the husband — until night falls and she readies the implements of the crime.
  • Soon
    Chapter 22 — Psyche sees Cupid
    In the lamplight Psyche sees not a monster but Cupid himself; in terror she turns the knife on herself — then, gazing, falls upon the beauty of the sleeping god.
  • Soon
    Chapter 23 — The lamp betrays her
    Marvelling at his wings and golden hair, Psyche toys with one of his own arrows — and the lamp spills a drop of burning oil onto the god's shoulder.
  • Soon
    Chapter 24 — Carried into the air
    Burned, Cupid leaps up and flies off; Psyche clings to his leg and is carried aloft, then dropped — and the god lectures her from a cypress before departing.
  • Soon
    Chapter 25 — Cupid flies away
    Psyche watches her husband disappear into the sky and, in despair, throws herself into the nearest river — which gently sets her down on the bank.
  • Soon
    Chapters 26–27 — The sisters' undoing
    Reunited with a sister, Psyche tells the disaster; the jealous sister rushes to take her place — and leaps from the crag to a god who is no longer waiting.
