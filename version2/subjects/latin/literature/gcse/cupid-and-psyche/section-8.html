1
Psyche meanwhile, flung down on the ground and, watching the flight of her husband as far as she could with her eyes, tormented her heart with the most intense laments.
2
But when the length of space had made her husband no longer hers, carried off by the oarage of his wings, headlong herself through she flung the edge of a river close by.
3
But the gentle stream, in honour of the god, of course, who is accustomed to set on fire even its very own waters, fearing for itself, at once her with a harmless eddy set her down onto a bank rich with grass.
Content — what is happening
  • Flung down on the ground, Psyche watches her husband’s flight as far as her eyes can follow, tormenting her heart with the bitterest of laments.
  • But when distance has carried him out of her sight — rowed away on the oarage of his wings — she hurls herself headlong over the brink of a nearby river.
  • Yet the gentle stream, out of reverence for the god whose fire can set even water ablaze, and fearing for itself, at once lifts her up on a harmless eddy and lays her down on a grassy bank.
