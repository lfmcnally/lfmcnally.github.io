1
Then Psyche, (though) otherwise weak in both body and mind, nevertheless, by the savagery of Fate lending aid, she is strengthened with strength, and, having brought out a lamp and seized a razor, she changed her sex by her daring.
2
But as soon as the secrets of the couch became clear, with the offering of light, she sees the gentlest and sweetest beast, of all wild beasts, that famous Cupid himself, a handsome god, reclining handsomely.
3
At the sight of him even cheerful the light of the lamp grew stronger and the razor regretted profane of its sharpness
4
But Psyche, however, terrified by such a great sight and powerless over her mind, drained, with languid paleness and trembling, sank down onto the bottom of her knees and she seeks to hide the blade — but in her own breast.
5
She would surely have done which if ... had not the blade, in fear of such a great disgrace, having slipped reckless from her rash had not flown away.
Content — what is happening
  • Weak in body and mind but driven on by the cruelty of Fate, Psyche nerves herself: she brings out the lamp, seizes the razor, and ‘changes her sex’ in daring.
  • The lamplight reveals the secret of the bed — and it is no monster but the gentlest, sweetest of all creatures: Cupid himself, a beautiful god lying beautifully asleep.
  • Even the lamp burns brighter at the sight, and the razor seems to repent its profane edge.
  • Terror-struck and faint, Psyche sinks to her knees and tries to hide the blade — in her own breast.
  • She would have killed herself, had the knife not slipped in fear from her reckless hands and flown away.
