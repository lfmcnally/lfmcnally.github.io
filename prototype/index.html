The redesign, in one screen

A clickable mock of the target navigation — no login, demo data only.

Today Classicalia is two disconnected worlds: a marketing site (subjects) and a separate app (tracking). This prototype shows them merged into one shell:

  • One nav, everywhere. The navy rail is always present — learning content and your progress live in the same place. No jumping to a different site.
  • Your courses are home. Login lands on My courses (the tracked_courses spine made visible), not a dense dashboard.
  • Every course has four verbs: Learn (content) · Practise (drills & games) · Assess (things that get marked) · Progress. Any future feature slots under one of these — that’s what stops the sprawl.
  • “Assessment” means one thing with three clear modes: Drill, Practice, Test — replacing today’s five overlapping surfaces.
  • Teachers get the mirror: flip to Teacher view (bottom-left) — one Assign button instead of four authoring flows.

Try: open GCSE Latin → click Assess. Then flip to Teacher view → Assign.
