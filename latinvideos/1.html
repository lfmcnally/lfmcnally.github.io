Summary
Key points

1st conjugation verbs have the infinitive ending -āre (e.g. portāre, to carry). Remove -re to find the stem: portā-.

The six present tense endings are -ō, -ās, -at, -āmus, -ātis, -ant. Each ending identifies the subject — no separate pronoun is needed.

The 1st person singular contracts: portā- + -ō → portō. All other persons keep the long -ā- of the stem.

The Latin present tense covers three English forms: simple (carries), continuous (is carrying), and emphatic (does carry). Context decides which fits.
