Passive Voice Review

Present Passive

Formed by adding passive endings to the present stem: -r, -ris, -tur, -mur, -mini, -ntur

laudatur = he/she is (being) praised
puella tacēbat = the girl was being touched

Imperfect Passive

Formed by adding passive endings to the imperfect stem: -bar, -bāris, -bātur, -bāmur, -bāmini, -bantur

laudābātur = he/she was being praised
castra construēbantur = the camp was being built

Future Passive

1st/2nd conjugation: -bor, -beris, -bitur, -bimur, -bimini, -buntur

3rd/4th conjugation: -ar, -ēris, -ētur, -ēmur, -ēmini, -entur

laudābitur = he/she will be praised
castra mittentur = the camp will be sent

Agent and Instrument

Agent (who does it): ā/ab + ablative

ā rēge laudātur = he is praised by the king

Instrument (with what): ablative alone

gladiō necātus est = he was killed with a sword
