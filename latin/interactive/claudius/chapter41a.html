Nero Enters Public Life - Chapter 41a

1a Ti. 2a Claudio 3a quintum 4a Servio 5a Cornelio 6a Orfito 7a consulibus 8a virilis 9a toga 10a Neroni 11a maturata 12a quo 13a capessendae 14a rei 15a publicae 16a habilis 17a videretur . 1b et 2b Caesar 3b adulationibus 4b senatus 5b libens 6b cessit 7b ut 8b vicesimo 9b aetatis 10b anno 11b consulatum 12b Nero 13b iniret 14b atque 15b interim 16b designatus 17b proconsulare 18b imperium 19b extra 20b urbem 21b haberet 22b ac 23b princeps 24b iuventutis 25b appellaretur . 1c additum 2c nomine 3c eius 4c donativum 5c militi , 6c congiarium 7c plebei . 1d et 2d ludicro 3d circensium , 4d quod 5d adquirendis 6d vulgi 7d studiis 8d edebatur , 9d Britannicus 10d in 11d praetexta , 12d Nero 13d triumphali 14d veste 15d travecti 16d sunt : 1e spectaret 2e populus 3e hunc 4e decore 5e imperatorio , 6e illum 7e puerili 8e habitu , 9e ac 10e perinde 11e fortunam 12e utriusque 13e praesumeret .
Chapter 41a Translation: In the consulships of Tiberius Claudius - for the fifth time - and Servius Cornelius Orfitus, the (granting of the) manly toga to Nero was accelerated, so that he might seem suitable to undertake a public role. Also, the emperor willingly yielded to the flattering suggestions of the Senate that Nero should enter upon the consulship in the 20th year of his life and in the meantime, as consul designate, he should hold proconsular power outside the city and be called leader of the youth. A donative was added for the soldiers in Nero's name, (and) a handout for the plebs. Also, at a performance of the circus games, which was provided in order to get the goodwill of the mob, Britannicus rode past in the toga praetexta, Nero in triumphal clothing: (the reasoning was that) the people should look at the latter in his triumphal glory and the former in boyhood apparel and would therefore anticipate the fortune of both.
