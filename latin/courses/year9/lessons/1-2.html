LESSON 1.2

Present Tense: porto

First conjugation verbs in the present tense

Learning Objectives
  • Understand how Latin verbs are built from stems and endings
  • Learn the present tense forms of first conjugation verbs
  • Recognise and translate present tense verb forms in context

Understanding Latin Verbs

Latin verbs work like building blocks. Every verb has two main parts: a stem (which gives the meaning) and an ending (which tells us who is doing the action). This system is much more efficient than English - where we need separate words like "I carry" or "they carry", Latin just changes the ending of one word.

Let's explore this with porto, which means "I carry". Notice how the stem port- stays constant while the endings change to show different people.

How to Build Verb Forms

1
Find the stem
Take the infinitive portare (to carry) and remove -are to get port-
2
Add the connecting vowel
First conjugation verbs use -a- as a connecting vowel (except for the 1st person singular)
3
Add the personal ending
The endings are: -≈ç, -s, -t, -mus, -tis, -nt
Remember: Latin can express both "I carry" and "I am carrying" with the same form - porto. Context tells us which meaning fits best.

Key Takeaways
  • Latin verbs consist of a stem + connecting vowel + personal ending
  • First conjugation verbs use -a- as the connecting vowel
  • Personal endings show who is doing the action: -≈ç, -s, -t, -mus, -tis, -nt
  • One Latin verb form can translate as "I carry" or "I am carrying"
