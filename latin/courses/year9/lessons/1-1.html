The Journey of Latin Through Time

Click on each era to explore Latin's incredible story

753 BC
The Birth of Rome
Latin begins as the language of a small Italian tribe. From humble beginnings on seven hills by the Tiber River, it will grow to shape the world.
100 BC - 200 AD
The Golden & Silver Ages
Caesar, Cicero, Virgil, Ovid - the greatest writers in history create masterpieces that will be read for millennia. Latin becomes the language of an empire spanning three continents.
500 - 1500 AD
Medieval Latin
Latin evolves into the universal language of learning, law, and faith. Every educated person in Europe thinks, writes, and dreams in Latin.
1500 - 1800
Scientific Revolution
Newton writes Principia Mathematica in Latin. Linnaeus creates the system for naming every living thing - in Latin. The language of the Romans becomes the language of discovery.
Today
Latin Lives On
From Harry Potter spells to legal terms, from medical terminology to your school motto - Latin surrounds us, shapes our languages, and continues to train the brightest minds.

Did You Know?

FUN FACT #1

The Harry Potter spells are almost all based on Latin! "Expecto Patronum" means "I await a protector," "Lumos" comes from "lumen" (light), and "Nox" means "night." Learning Latin is like unlocking a secret code in your favourite books and films!

FUN FACT #2

Romans didn't use spaces between words! THEYWROTELIKETHIS. They also didn't have lowercase letters - everything was in capitals. Punctuation? They barely used it. Reading Latin was a real skill!

FUN FACT #3

Latin is the official language of Vatican City, making it the only country where Latin is still actively used for official documents. The Vatican even has a Latin cash machine - imagine withdrawing money in the language of the Romans!
