Easy Translation Trick
The dative can almost always be translated with "to" or "for" in English.

Think about these English sentences:

  • I give a present to my brother
  • She shows the way to the sailor
  • We tell the story to the girl

The words after "to" would be in the dative case in Latin!

The Pattern
Subject (nominative) + Indirect Object (dative) + Direct Object (accusative) + Verb
üí° A note on word order

Latin word order is flexible, but it's very common to see the dative before the accusative. Think of it as: who you're giving TO comes before what you're giving.

How to Spot the Dative

Here's a simple process for identifying the dative case in a sentence:

Step 1: Find the verb

Example: mƒÅter senƒ´ pec≈´niam dat

The verb is dat (gives) - a verb that takes the dative!

Step 2: Ask "to whom?" or "for whom?"

Question: The mother gives money to whom?

Step 3: Check the ending

The ending -ƒ´ is the 3rd declension dative singular. Perfect!

Quick Tips
Look for verbs like "give" and "tell" - they almost always have a dative object. If you can add "to" or "for" before a word in English, it's probably dative in Latin!
