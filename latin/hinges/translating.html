Tempting, but risky

This is a natural instinct ‚Äî it's how we read English, after all. But Latin word order is much freer than English. The first word of a Latin sentence might be an adjective, a prepositional phrase, or a subordinate conjunction. If you start translating word by word from the left, you'll often end up confused or producing something that doesn't make sense in English. You need to find the structure of the sentence first, and that means dealing with the prepositional phrases before anything else.

2
Find the main verb and work out what tense it is.
Right idea, wrong order

Finding the verb is essential ‚Äî it's one of the most important steps. And with irregular perfects, identifying the tense correctly is a real skill. So this answer shows good instinct. The problem is that if you go straight to the verb in a long sentence, you might accidentally pick up a verb from a subordinate clause or get distracted by prepositional phrases. Bracketing the prepositional phrases first strips away the clutter so you can see the main verb more clearly.

3
Bracket the prepositional phrases to clear them out of the way.
That's right ‚úì

Bracketing prepositional phrases (like ad templum, in via, e foro) is the best first move. These phrases are self-contained chunks: a preposition plus its noun in the accusative or ablative. Once you bracket them, the sentence looks much simpler and you can see the core structure ‚Äî subject, verb, object ‚Äî far more easily. From there, you find the subject (nominative noun or verb ending), then the verb, then the object, and finally slot the bracketed phrases back in.

4
Find the subject by looking for a nominative noun.
Close ‚Äî this is step two

Finding the subject is a crucial part of the method, so this answer shows you're thinking along the right lines. The issue is that in a long sentence, there might be several nominative nouns ‚Äî one in the main clause, one in a relative clause, perhaps another in a quod clause. If you haven't cleared the prepositional phrases out of the way first, it's harder to tell which nominative belongs to the main clause. Bracket first, then find the subject ‚Äî that's the order that keeps things clear.
