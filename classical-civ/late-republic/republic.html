The Roman Republic: How It Worked

509–27 BC Foundations

How did the Roman Republic's political system function, and what held it together?

Why This Page Matters

This page is not a chronological topic to revise but a permanent reference. Every event in the late Republic — from the Gracchi to Caesar's assassination — only makes sense if you understand the constitutional system that was being broken, bent, or defended. Return to this page whenever you need to check how a magistracy worked, what mos maiorum meant in practice, or why the optimates and populares were methods rather than parties.

The Republic was not a democracy. It was an aristocratic oligarchy run by a narrow senatorial elite, held together by custom, religion, and social obligation rather than a written constitution. Understanding this is essential for every essay you will write.
