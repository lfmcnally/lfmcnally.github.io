Marius and Sulla

107–78 BC Marius Sulla

Did Marius and Sulla destroy the Republic between them, or were they products of its failures?

Why This Page Matters

Marius and Sulla represent the moment when the Republic's political conflicts become military conflicts. Marius's reforms create armies loyal to their general rather than the state. Sulla's march on Rome in 88 BC proves that the army can be used as a political weapon. Everything that follows — Pompey, Caesar, the civil wars — builds on the template Marius and Sulla create.
