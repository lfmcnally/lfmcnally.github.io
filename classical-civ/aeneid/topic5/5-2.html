Allecto's Three-Stage Attack

Allecto infects Queen Amata (domestic power), inflames Turnus (military power), and engineers the deer incident (popular rage). War starts from manufactured madness, not rational conflict.

The Pattern of Infection
Allecto amplifies existing emotions‚ÄîAmata's preference becomes frenzy, Turnus's confidence becomes rage. She doesn't create new feelings, just weaponizes natural ones. That's what makes it tragic.

Infecting Amata

Allecto throws a snake into Amata's heart. The venom spreads gradually‚Äîfirst rational argument, then Bacchic frenzy. She leads Latin women to the mountains, hiding Lavinia and undermining Latinus's authority.

For Exams

The snake imagery = hidden corruption spreading invisibly. Amata doesn't know she's being manipulated‚Äîshe thinks her rage is justified. That's the power of furor.

Inflaming Turnus

Allecto disguises as old priestess Calybe, urging Turnus to war. When he dismisses her (pride, sexism), she reveals her true form and hurls a torch into his heart. Instant transformation‚Äîlust for weapons, war-madness, rage.

Boiling Pot Simile
Virgil compares Turnus to sealed pot boiling over‚Äîpressure builds until explosion inevitable. Furor removes agency; violence feels natural, unstoppable.

The Deer Incident

Allecto guides Ascanius's hounds to Silvia's pet deer. He shoots it. The wounded deer returns home bleeding. Silvia screams. Farmers rush to defend her‚Äîarmed conflict erupts. First blood: Almo (Tyrrhus's son) and Galaesus (the most righteous man, trying to make peace).

Why This Matters

Great tragedies have trivial triggers. War starts over a hunting accident. The innocent die first‚Äîdefenders and peacemakers. Righteousness doesn't protect you in war.
