Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will have detailed knowledge of Book 12, including the broken treaty, the divine settlement, and the final duel between Aeneas and Turnus.

Book 12: The End

Book 12 brings the epic to its controversial close. Turnus and Aeneas agree to single combat; the treaty is broken; battle resumes; finally the duel takes place. Turnus falls, begs for mercy, and Aeneas‚Äîseeing Pallas's belt‚Äîkills him in fury. The poem ends abruptly.

Treaty and Betrayal

Turnus's Decision

Turnus, cornered, accepts single combat: "I will meet the Trojan; let the war end with me." His mother Amata and Lavinia (blushing, weeping) try to dissuade him. Turnus is magnificent in his resolve‚Äîtragic in his doom.

The Treaty

Aeneas and Latinus swear oaths: if Turnus wins, the Trojans leave; if Aeneas wins, Trojans and Latins will unite as equals‚ÄîTrojans will "subside into Latins," keeping customs but losing their name. The terms are generous; peace is near.

Juturna's Intervention

Juno sends Juturna, Turnus's divine sister (a river nymph), to break the truce. Disguised as a warrior, she stirs up the Latins: why let one man decide their fate? A soldier throws a spear; the truce shatters.

Renewed Battle

Battle resumes. Aeneas tries to stop it, calling for the treaty‚Äîbut is wounded by an arrow. Venus heals him with magical herbs; he returns to fight. Juturna saves Turnus repeatedly, driving his chariot away from Aeneas.

The Attack on Laurentum

Aeneas, unable to find Turnus, attacks the Latin city itself. The women mount the walls to defend; fire threatens. Amata, believing Turnus dead, hangs herself. The city's fall seems imminent.

Divine Resolution

Jupiter's Ultimatum

Jupiter confronts Juno: enough. She has harried the Trojans enough. The outcome is fixed; she cannot prevent it. What does she want?

Juno's Terms

Juno accepts defeat but extracts conditions: the Trojans must lose their name; Latins must keep their language and customs; there must be no second Troy. Jupiter agrees: "The Romans will arise, mixed from both, surpassing all in piety to you."

The Settlement's Meaning

This divine bargain establishes Roman identity: not purely Trojan but Trojan-Italian fusion. Juno's hatred is appeased; she will become a Roman goddess. The cosmic opposition ends; Rome's future is secured.

The Dira

Jupiter sends a Dira (Fury/monster) to terrify Turnus and drive off Juturna. The Dira appears as an owl of death, beating against Turnus's shield. His strength drains; his sister recognises the sign and leaves him to die.

Sermonem Ausonii patrium moresque tenebunt... Hinc genus Ausonio mixtum quod sanguine surget...

The Ausonians shall keep their ancestral speech and customs... From this shall arise a race mixed with Ausonian blood...

The Final Duel

The Combat

Aeneas and Turnus finally meet. They fight with swords; Turnus's sword shatters (he had grabbed the wrong one in his haste). He flees; Aeneas pursues. Turnus throws a boulder‚Äîit falls short. The Dira has drained his strength.

Aeneas's Spear

Aeneas's spear strikes Turnus in the thigh. He falls, wounded but alive. He raises his hand in supplication: "I have deserved this. Use your fortune. But if any care for a father's grief can touch you‚Äîmy father Daunus is old‚Äîgive me back to my people."

The Moment of Decision

Aeneas hesitates. He is moved. He might have spared Turnus. But then he sees Pallas's belt on Turnus's shoulder. Memory of his friend's death surges back. Fury kindles.

The Killing

"Are you, wearing the spoils of my dear one, to be snatched from me? Pallas strikes this blow, Pallas sacrifices you with this wound!" Aeneas plunges his sword into Turnus's chest. Turnus's limbs go cold; his life flees groaning to the shades below.

The Ending
The poem ends here‚Äîabruptly, on Turnus's death. No celebration, no wedding, no founding of cities. Just anger and killing. This ending has troubled readers for two thousand years. Does Aeneas fail his own virtue? Or does he fulfill pietas through vengeance? The ambiguity is deliberate and unresolved.
